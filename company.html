The Aurora Story

2017

Company Founded

Aurora was founded by Chris Urmson (former lead of Google's self-driving car project), Sterling Anderson (former director of Tesla Autopilot), and Drew Bagnell (former Uber ATG autonomy and perception lead).

2019

Major Investment

Aurora secures over $530 million in Series B funding led by Sequoia, with significant investment from Amazon and T. Rowe Price.

2021

Strategic Acquisition

Aurora acquires Uber's self-driving unit (Uber ATG) and announces plans to focus on autonomous trucking as its first commercial application.

2021

Public Company

Aurora goes public via a SPAC merger with Reinvent Technology Partners Y, valuing the company at $13 billion.

2023

Commercial Launch

Aurora launches its first commercial autonomous trucking routes between Dallas and Houston, partnering with major logistics providers.

2025

Today

Aurora's autonomous trucks operate regular commercial routes across the Sun Belt, with expanding operations and partnerships with leading trucking companies.

About Aurora

Aurora is a leader in self-driving technology, developing the Aurora Driver—an autonomous driving system designed for integration into trucks and passenger vehicles. The company has focused its commercial strategy on long-haul trucking, addressing critical challenges in the logistics industry including driver shortages, safety concerns, and operational inefficiencies. Aurora's autonomous trucks are designed to operate on highways and interstates for extended periods without driver intervention, using a sophisticated suite of sensors, software, and hardware. The company partners with major truck manufacturers including Volvo, PACCAR (Peterbilt and Kenworth), and logistics providers to deploy its technology at scale. With safety as its core principle, Aurora employs a methodical, evidence-based approach to autonomous vehicle development and commercialization, differentiating itself through its focus on long-term technical excellence rather than short-term deadlines.

Chris Urmson, widely recognized as a pioneer in autonomous vehicle technology, co-founded Aurora after leading Google's self-driving car project (now Waymo) for seven years. With a Ph.D. in robotics from Carnegie Mellon University and experience leading teams that won the DARPA Grand Challenge, Urmson brings unparalleled technical expertise and leadership to Aurora. His vision for autonomous vehicles is grounded in a methodical, safety-first approach that values rigorous engineering and systematic validation over rushed deadlines. Under his guidance, Aurora has strategically positioned itself as a technology provider focused on solving the most challenging aspects of autonomous driving, particularly for commercial applications like long-haul trucking where the technology can address critical industry needs and provide clear economic benefits. Urmson's leadership has attracted top talent from across the autonomous vehicle industry, creating a company culture that emphasizes technical excellence and long-term impact.

Transforming Logistics

As of 2025, Aurora's self-driving truck technology has evolved into a comprehensive solution addressing the unique challenges of long-haul trucking. The Aurora Driver integrates powerful sensors—including Aurora's proprietary FirstLight Lidar, radar, and cameras—with advanced perception software capable of detecting and tracking objects at highway speeds in diverse weather and lighting conditions. This technology enables Aurora-powered trucks to operate safely on interstate highways, navigating complex traffic scenarios, construction zones, and unexpected obstacles. The system is designed for seamless integration with commercial truck platforms from manufacturers like Volvo and PACCAR, allowing for efficient scaling and deployment. Aurora's virtual testing suite allows the company to continuously refine its technology by simulating millions of driving scenarios for every software release, supplementing real-world testing with extensive virtual validation. Through strategic partnerships with major logistics providers, Aurora is operating commercial routes primarily across the Sun Belt states, focusing initially on hub-to-hub transportation where autonomous trucks handle the highway portions of journeys while human drivers manage first and last mile operations in urban areas. This approach maximizes the impact of autonomous technology while acknowledging its current limitations.
